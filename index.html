our story

Family first. Always has been.

Tucked away on Toulouse Street, Maria Old Age Home has become a second family for the elders who live here — and for the families who trust us with them. We believe growing older should never mean growing lonely.

Our days are unhurried. There's tea in the lounge, sunshine on the lawn, hymns on a Sunday, and always someone nearby to hold a hand, share a story, or simply sit a while. Whether a resident needs a wheelchair, a walking stick, or just good company, our caregivers are there — patient, present, and proud of the work they do.

We're a small home with a big heart, and we'd love for you to come see it for yourself.

— with love, the Maria Old Age Home family

our friends

Held up by a caring community

We are endlessly grateful to the local businesses and organisations who visit, donate and spend time with our residents throughout the year.

Air Liquide's Mandela Day visit

The Air Liquide team spent the day with us, handing out warm blankets and groceries to keep our residents cosy through winter.

Teksa Alarms give-back day

Teksa Alarms — Service to People — joined us on the lawn for an afternoon of gifts, laughter and good conversation.

thank you ♥

To every donor, volunteer and visitor: your blankets, groceries and time mean more to our residents than words can say. If your organisation would like to spend a day with us, we'd love to have you.
